Name: Margeret

Age: 42

Gender: Female

Occupation: Travel agent

Technology: Margeret mainly uses the latest iPhone, but she also uses a windows desktop computer when she has free time between meetings with clients at her job as a travel agent.

Operating systems: Windows 10 and iOS 13

Frustrations and Needs: Margeret is a middle aged suburban mother of two children aged 8 and 12. She is looking for a pet for her family, but due to the fact that her and her husband both work, they are looking for a pet that is able to be left alone for some period of time, they are also looking for a dog that is big enough to act as a guard dog.

Margeret would love to adopt a dog for her family. She has heard horror stories from neighbors about destructive or aggressive dogs that had to be returned to the animal shelter, she would like to able to find a dog that has the right temperament. Margeret's main frustration is that it is very time consuming to sift through various shelter websites in order to find the right dog.

User Story:Margeret should be able to quickly and easily look through information on dogs that are available for adoption from various shelters on a single website and bookmark the dogs that she is interested in adopting.

Interaction flow:

  • Margeret enters her log-in information on the initial splash screen.
  • The browser receives her information and verifies that her username and password are correct; she is logged in to her account.
  • Margeret is taken to a page with a large image of an animal that is in a shelter.
  • She is not interested in adopting the animal that is displayed. She selects the option that sends information that she is not interested in adopting the animal shown.
  • The server receives the information that the user is not interested in that animal, and the animal is tagged as "not interested" in the database. The browser then sends a photo of another animal that is up for adoption.
  • She is not interested in adopting the next animal that is displayed, the above process repeats several times.
  • When Margeret sees a photo of an animal that she is interested in adopting, she selects an option that shows that she is interested in adopting the animal that is displayed.
  • The server receives the input from the user that they are interested in adopting the animal that is displayed, the animal that the user was viewing is then tagged as "interested" in the database. The browser then displays another animal for the user to consider.
  • When Margeret is finished viewing animals she click on her profile.
  • The browser displays her profile information.
  • Margeret clicks on an option that shows a list of animals that she is interested in adopting.
  • The browser displays profiles of all of the animals that Margeret has tagged as "interested" in the database.
  • Margeret then clicks on the profiles of the individual animals.
  • The browser then displays the profile information for the individual animals that Margeret clicks on. Each profile has information on the animal as well as the contact information for the shelter that they are housed in.
  • Margeret contacts a shelter to begin the adoption process for an animal.
